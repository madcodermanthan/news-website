LONDON: Britain launched an investigation on Tuesday (Oct 1) into the auditors who signed off on accounts for the now-bankrupt holiday giant Thomas Cook, as the company's French branch went into receivership and its Belgian division collapsed. The Financial Reporting Council said in a statement that it will examine whether accountancy firm EY - which replaced PwC as Thomas Cook's auditors in 2017 - acted properly in scrutinising its books.
